Our Story

Savon Forestry Services Ltd was established as a private nature-based solution enterprise to address pressing environmental, soil, and water conservation challenges in Northern Uganda. With our team of dedicated experts, we provide comprehensive forestry and environmental services.

Our Mission

To provide sustainable environmental solutions that conserve and enhance Northern Uganda's natural resources while supporting local communities and economies.

Our Values

  • Sustainability: We prioritize long-term environmental health in all our activities.
  • Quality: We maintain the highest standards in our seeds, seedlings, and services.
  • Knowledge: We believe in sharing expertise through education and training.
  • Innovation: We implement modern techniques for effective conservation solutions.

Our Impact

Through our efforts in Northern Uganda, we have established numerous woodlots, distributed thousands of seedlings, and trained local communities in sustainable forestry practices, contributing to environmental preservation and economic development.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about our forestry and environmental services.

What tree species do you offer?

We offer a variety of indigenous and exotic tree species suited to Northern Uganda's climate, including Teak, Pine, Eucalyptus, Mahogany, Musizi, and various fruit trees. Our selection is based on environmental suitability and client needs.

How do I maintain my trees after planting?

Regular watering, weeding, and monitoring for pests are essential in the first few years. We provide detailed care instructions specific to your tree species and offer ongoing management services if needed.

Do you offer services for small residential properties?

Yes, we offer landscaping and small-scale tree planting services for residential properties. Our experts can help design and implement beautiful, environmentally-friendly garden spaces regardless of size.

How long does it take to establish a woodlot?

The timeline varies depending on tree species and objectives. Fast-growing species like Eucalyptus may yield results in 5-7 years, while hardwoods like Teak require 15-20 years. We help you develop a plan based on your timeframe and goals.
